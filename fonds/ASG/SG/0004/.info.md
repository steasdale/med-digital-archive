# San Giorgio 4

## Archival Path

ASG > San Giorgio > Status, ordinamento, memorie  > Contratti e privilegi

## Titles

*Contractuum 1453 in 1476. B* (cover).

*Liber primus privilegiorum. B* (back cover).

## Description

This archival item is a book of rights and privileges belonging to the Casa di San Giorgio, derived from consolidated purchases or acquired directly, and of the contracts it entered into with the political authorities, undated but from 1332 to *post*-1479.

The records, which cover transactions carried out from May 12, 1332, to September 16, 1479, consist of documents originally drafted in the book and signed by the municipal clerks, or of simple or certified copies. They concern rights and privileges of every kind that the Casa obtained (mostly in exchange for financing) from the Genoese state, from the *pro tempore* prince (the Dukes of Milan in folios 70, 74, 98, 129, 131, 132; the King of France at fol. 56) and, more rarely, from the Pope (fol. 23, privilege of Callixtus III, incomplete), the Emperor, or other monarchs (fol. 18, Renatus, King of Jerusalem and Sicily; at fol. 128, the Doge of Venice). Contracts with the state are sometimes preceded by the minutes of the meetings of its collegial bodies. On folio 26: drawing; on pp. 3 and 4 of the cover (that is, series II): maxims. The insert is a nineteenth-century transcription of the document referred to in folios 1–4.

Single manuscript, 165 folios (three series, fols. 1v–8r; fols. 1r–24v, 26r–158v; fols 9r–16v), 375 x 270 mm; one insert.; leather and wood cover with metal studs. Only the inner half of the back cover remains; folio 25 has been cut out.

For an index of the documents contained in the register, see San Giorgio 80 (fols. 1r–6v).
